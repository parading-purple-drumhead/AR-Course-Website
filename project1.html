Project 1 - Around The World

Introduction

This project was made as a part of the coursework in CS 428 Virtual, Augmented and Mixed Reality, instructed by Dr. Andrew Johnson at the University of Illinois at Chicago during the Fall 2022 semester. The aim of the project was to create two AR-based knickknacks of two different attractions of our choice and also display the local time and live weather of the attractions. My choice of attractions for the knickknack were Chicago's Cloud Gate and the United Center. Chicago is a city I've always wanted to live in or at least visit, with seeing The Bean being on my bucket list. Having been a Bulls fan ever since I started watching basketball, attending a game at the United Center is another bucket list activity.

Running the Project

Prerequisites
  • Unity Hub and Unity (Version 2021.3.6f1) - Download Resource
  • Vuforia (Version 10.9 or greater) - Download Resource
  • Android Phone (running Oreo 8.0 and above)
  • Printed, Cut and Folded Merge cube - Download: Merge Cube
Installing The Project
  1. Open the following link to the GitHub repository for this project:
    428.P1.Sudhanshu GitHub Repository
  2. Click on the green Code button and then Download ZIP
  3. On your PC, extract the folder to your desired directory
  4. Open Unity Hub, click on Open and navigate to the extracted project folder and click Open again.
    Make sure your Unity version is 2021.3.6f1
  5. An error should appear, as seen on the right, due to the project being misconfigured. Click Continue in the dialog box.
  6. Another dialog box asking to enter the project in Safe Mode should appear. Click Ignore to continue. We shall be resolving this error shortly.
  7. Within the Unity window, go to the Scenes folder under Assets in the project files explorer on the bottom-left of the window, as shown. Drag Sample Scene and replace Untitled in the project Hierarchy on the top left.
    If the Untitled scene still exists, right-click and click Remove Scene.
  8. Next, download Vuforia from the link below. Ensure the version selected in 10.9 or above. Click on Add Vuforia Engine to a Unity Project or upgrade to the latest version.

    Vuforia: Download Resource
  9. Open the downloaded file by double-clicking it. This opens the Import Unity Package window. Click Import.
  10. Click on the Help tab on the top-left and then click Reset Packages to Default.
  11. A prompt asking to Add Vuforia will appear. Click Update
  12. Upon completion of the above steps, the project should look like this
  13. Now click on the AR Camera in the Hierarchy on the left and then scroll down in the Inspector on the right and click on Open Vuforia Configuration
  14. Replace the key in the Add License Key box with your own developer license key which can be generated at the link below after signing up.

    Vuforia License Key: Vuforia License Manager
  15. Towards the top-center of the editor, click on the (Play) button. Hold up the Merge Cube, and the AR knickknack should appear on your screen as shown.

AR Eyewear In The Future

The scope for the existence of AR objects such as the knickknacks made in this project is high, given how the technology for wearables is getting better with each passing day. While the techbology is still too rudimentary to be implemented now, the very fact that the foundations for such ideas are set gives hope to future AR enthusiasts and developers. The array of applications for small AR objects is vast given how making glanceable pieces of information handy and interactive automatically piques the reader's interest a lot more than a piece of flat text would.

Museums keep looking for new ways to make education for children more interactive and interesting. Holographic displays are already widely implemented in musuems for this very purpose. Small AR interactive objects only add to the learning experience here.

However there is a flip side to this. For such wearable AR technology, the cost is high and the availability is low. This makes AR experiences like these accessible to mostly first world countries and even there to the upper sects of society. Factors like these vastly affect just how common everyday AR objects might get. They are intriguing, but hard to get your hands on.

In terms of what objects could be made popular, mini-games, match scores, AR clocks, etc, could be among those popularized by AR glasses.
